Appeal Brief — One-Page Summary

The appeal challenges the approval of a high-volume mosque at 9600 2nd Street NW, citing lack of project-specific traffic analysis, procedural violations, and inadequate evidence supporting safety and neighborhood impact findings. Key issues include deferral of traffic studies to permit stage, inconsistent parking and attendance data, and failure to address regional corridor capacity, with requests for remand, comprehensive traffic review, and policy reforms.

12Preliminary Trip Generation Assessment — From the recordPreliminary trip-generation assessment prepared by the Appeal Coalition (not Terry O. Brown) and placed on the record before the July 1, 2026 hearing — a screening-level analysis compiled solely from record-based inputs: the Applicant's own data, the record, and the 2020 Murphy Express Traffic Study (the only traffic study in the record, created and stamped by Terry O. Brown, P.E.).
  • What it shows: ITE-style screening on the Board-imposed 60,000-SF cap projects ~240–300 peak-hour trips, cross-checked against the Appellant's record-based weekly table (Exhibit 14: 2,164 weekly / 560 Friday).
  • Threshold exceeded: Both analyses exceed the County's DPM Ch. 7 § 7-5(C)(2) 100-peak-hour-trip TIA threshold by multiples — confirming a formal, engineer-prepared TIA was required before conditional-use approval, and deferral could not retroactively supply the substantial evidence Section 24 requires.
  • Whose duty it was: A traffic screening answers a threshold land-use question — whether the site's trip generation fits the location and the corridor's documented capacity. That question was the Applicant's to answer; it was mandatory and required little effort (the Appellant compiled this one from the Applicant's own data already in the record).
  • County's failure: The Applicant provided none; the County did not hold the Applicant accountable and instead pushed the project through without the mandatory evidence — leaving the Appellant to supply, on the community's behalf, the very screening the County should have required. The County should not have put the Appellant in that position. The screening demonstrates that the proposed use does not fit this location and flags the need for a formal, project-specific TIA before — not after — approval, exactly the determination the County was obligated to make before authorizing the land use.

  • Directs the County to “limit access to Second Street, Alameda Boulevard, and Montaño Road” from adjacent properties except where alternative access is unavailable or infeasible.

24Applicant's "Dear Neighbor" upgrade letter (postmarked April 6, 2026)The Applicant's own outreach letter to neighboring property owners, postmarked April 6, 2026 (same date as the stamped site plan, Sheet S-100, and the conceptual school drop-off/queuing exhibit, Sheet C-100). Included because it is the Applicant's own written representation to neighbors that:
  • Finding 1 mischaracterization: The Applicant itself told neighbors “our proposal includes a licensed daycare and K-12 school located entirely within the main building” — its own representation that the school/daycare were a core, licensed component, so Finding 1's framing of them as mere “incidental facilities” mischaracterizes the Applicant's own project description.
  • Informal assurance ≠ substantial evidence (and stall count is not a traffic study): The letter assures neighbors that “all school and congregational traffic remains safely on-site” because the site has 235 spaces, claimed as “more than double the county requirement.” But 235 is not in fact double the requirement (154 for worship, 205 with the school) — so his own representation overstates the ratio, and his own submissions elsewhere are irreconcilable (one states 350–400 attendees / 150–200 vehicles; another calculates off 250 attendees / 202 spaces — a 40–60% swing never reconciled on the record). More importantly, counting stalls says nothing about the actual Section 24 question: how many trips the site generates and whether Alameda / 2nd Street — already at Level of Service F per the Murphy study — can absorb them. Using stall count as a proxy for “traffic is handled” is precisely the kind of unsubstantiated assurance that cannot substitute for a TIA, and no TIA was ever produced.
